# SEO Content Standard

Every proposed page starts with a reader question and an evidence basis. A page is publishable only
when it offers a distinct answer not already served by an existing URL.

## Editorial template

1. Primary reader and question.
2. Direct answer in the opening paragraph.
3. Observed problem, with source where a factual claim is made.
4. What is known, assumed, illustrative, or unknown.
5. Regulus approach and human decision boundary.
6. Useful next step and descriptive internal links.
7. Unique title, description, canonical, H1, and appropriate JSON-LD.
8. Organizational author; honest publication and modification dates for articles.

## Quality checklist

- [ ] One page owns one primary intent; no keyword stuffing.
- [ ] One H1; descriptive heading hierarchy.
- [ ] No invented client, result, statistic, certification, location, or compliance claim.
- [ ] Operational services, development systems, research, and speculation remain distinct.
- [ ] Unknowns stay unknown; assumptions and illustrations are labelled.
- [ ] Meaningful images have plain-language alt text; decorative images use empty alt or aria-hidden.
- [ ] Links use descriptive anchor text and resolve.
- [ ] CTA matches the reader’s intent and does not promise an outcome.
- [ ] Metadata and schema describe visible content only.
- [ ] Human review completed before publication.
